    FAQ

    Common questions

    Is NutriTrack really free?
    Yes — completely free. No account needed, no credit card, no subscription. NutriTrack runs on AI APIs with no paywall in front of any feature.
    Where does the nutrient data come from?
    All nutrient reference values are based on the NIH Dietary Reference Intakes (DRI), which include Recommended Dietary Allowances (RDA) and Adequate Intakes (AI). Values are segmented by age group, gender, and adjusted for BMI and activity level.
    Can I trust the AI diagnosis?
    Dr. NutriAI provides a good starting point for understanding possible deficiencies based on your symptoms — and the confidence ratings (High / Medium / Low) help you gauge how seriously to take each flag. However, it is not a replacement for blood tests or professional medical diagnosis. Always consult a qualified healthcare provider for confirmation.
    How does the meal plan handle Indian diets?
    The AI is specifically prompted to generate Indian-first meal plans — mixing North Indian, South Indian, street food, and home-cooked meals. It covers dal varieties, sabzis, rice dishes, rotis, idli, dosa, chaats, and more, alongside continental options for variety.
    Is my data stored anywhere?
    No. All calculations happen in your browser. Your data is never sent to a server except for the AI API calls and the optional email step — neither of which stores anything after the response is returned.
